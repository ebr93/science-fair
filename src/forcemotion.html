Force and Motion

Everything that moves is affected by force. Force is a push or pull that can change an object's motion, like the kick you give to a ball or the wind pushing a kite. Scientists who study force and motion are like detectives, trying to figure out how forces affect objects. They use special tools to measure motion and conduct experiments to learn about the laws of motion. Their discoveries help us understand the world around us and create new technologies that make our lives easier.

1. Balloon Rocket

Question Explored: How does the force of air affect the motion of an object?

    Instructions:

  • 1. Cut a piece of string and thread it through the straw.

  • 2. Tie the string to two fixed points (e.g., chairs) or have a partner hold the ends.

  • 3. Blow up a balloon and tape it onto the straw.

  • 4. Release the balloon, and observe how the air escaping from the balloon propels the balloon rocket along the string.

    Materials:

  • - Balloons

  • - String

  • - Plastic straw

  • - Tape

  • - Measuring tape

    Observation Questions:

  • - What causes the balloon rocket to move?

  • - Did changing the size of the balloon affect how far the rocket traveled?

  • - How does the force of the air pushing out of the balloon affect the motion?

2. Egg Drop Challenge

Question Explored: How can forces of impact be reduced to protect an object during a fall?

    Instructions:

  • 1. Divide the class into groups and provide them with the materials.

  • 2. Each group designs a structure to protect the egg from breaking when dropped from a height.

  • 3. Test the structures by dropping them from the same height and observe the impact on the eggs.

    Materials:

  • - Raw eggs

  • - Various materials for padding (e.g., cotton balls, bubble wrap, cardboard, paper, tape)

  • - Small containers or bags for holding the eggs

    Observation Questions:

  • - Which materials and designs were most effective in protecting the egg?

  • - How did different structures absorb the impact forces?

  • - What forces were acting on the egg when it was dropped?

3. Paper Airplane Contest

Question Explored: How does the design of a paper airplane affect its flight?

    Instructions:

  • 1. Provide various types of paper and allow students to fold paper airplanes using any design they choose.

  • 2. Have a flying contest to see whose airplane goes the farthest or stays in the air the longest.

    Materials:

  • - Paper (preferably different types: printer paper, construction paper, etc.)

  • - Ruler

  • - Tape

    Observation Questions:

  • - What features of the paper airplane design affected its flight?

  • - Did changing the type of paper affect the flight characteristics?

  • - How did force and motion play a role in the airplanes' flight?

4. Pendulum Painting

Question Explored: How does the force of gravity affect the motion of a pendulum?

    Instructions:

  • 1. Attach one end of the string to a fixed point (like a hook or doorknob) and the other end to a small cup with paint.

  • 2. Swing the cup to create a pendulum, allowing it to move over the paper or canvas.

  • 3. Observe the patterns and designs made by the swinging cup.

    Materials:

  • - String

  • - Washable paint

  • - Small cups or containers

  • - Large paper or canvas

  • - Tape

    Observation Questions:

  • - What shapes and patterns did the pendulum create?

  • - How did the force of gravity impact the motion of the pendulum?

  • - What factors affected the patterns made by the pendulum?

5. Rubber Band-Powered Cars

Question Explored: How does the energy stored in a rubber band affect the motion of a car?

    Instructions:

  • 1. Build a small car by attaching wheels to a base (cardboard or foam board) and creating an axle using skewers or straws.

  • 2. Wind a rubber band around the rear axle and secure it.

  • 3. Release the rubber band and observe the car's motion.

    Materials:

  • - Wooden skewers or straws

  • - Bottle caps or small wheels

  • - Rubber bands

  • - Tape

  • - Cardboard or foam board

  • - Scissors

    Observation Questions:

  • - How does the energy stored in the rubber band create movement for the car?

  • - Did changing the size or number of rubber bands affect the car's motion?

  • - How did different designs or materials impact the car's speed or distance traveled?
